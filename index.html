About Me

After receiving a B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Carnegie Mellon in 2018, I moved to the beautiful Pacific Northwest to begin working as a Microsoft Program Manager. I work in Operating Systems security on application control technology, which prevents untrusted programs from running on Windows or Linux devices.

My Work

Application Control

For the past two years, I've been working at Microsoft as a Program Manager building security features that help customers defend against cyber attacks. Because I am on a highly technical team which traditionally works less on customer-facing features than I do, I have been given the opportunity and challenge to fill a number of roles: amongst them project manager, designer, user researcher, and technical writer.

I've grown quickly in my coordination skills from projects which have required me to gather dozens of subject matter experts (who were far my seniors, came from teams across the company, and often had contradictory opinions and goals) and move everyone towards a shared understanding. I've had to find answers to quandaries like what to do when I'm running a meeting in which two argumentative lead engineers have very different ideas about how to solve a problem about which I myself have limited knowledge. This has been a difficult but useful crash course in asking probing questions, encouraging respectful communication, and creating clear expectation-setting— even in environments where this is not the norm.

I've also had the chance to work directly with customers both to assist them in securing their environments and also to get meaningful qualitative feedback on my feature area. It's been extremely rewarding to hear about where our customers have succeeded in using my feature and knowing that I can over time design improvements for the pain points that get in their way. Application control is a complex problem space, so I've gotten to sit with the question of how to take something which is inherently complicated and try to make it approachable.
